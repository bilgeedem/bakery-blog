INGREDIENTS

for 4 portions

80 g butter
25 g powdered sugar
125 g flour
50 g almond(s) or nuts of your choice, grated
1 pinch(s) baking powder, generously measured
1 pinch(s) salt
1 egg
2 tbsp jam, preferably sour
175 g cream cheese
75 g cream
Sugar, as desired
125 g Berries, if desired
Powdered sugar or toasted almond flakes
PREPERATION

Mix flour almonds (nuts) and baking powder. Mix butter with powdered sugar & salt.
Separate the egg. Add the egg yolks to the butter and set the egg whites aside.
Mix the butter and egg yolks, then briefly knead in the flour mixture. Wrap the dough
in cling film and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

Preheat the oven to 200°C. After 30 minutes, line the molds with the dough and prick
the bottom of the tartlets several times with a fork. Bake the tartlets on the middle
rack for about 15 min. Whisk the egg white a little and after 15 minutes of baking,
carefully brush the inside of the tartlets with it. Bake for another 5-10 minutes
(depending on the desired degree of browning and the oven). Stir the jam until smooth
(if necessary with a few drops of hot water). Spread the tartlets with the jam
immediately after baking. Let cool down.

In the meantime, whip the cream until stiff. Stir the cream cheese until smooth and
sweeten to taste. Fold in the cream. Spread the cream over the cooled tartlets and
garnish with the berries. Before serving, decorate with a little powdered sugar or
a few toasted almond flakes.

INGREDIENTS

for 4 portions

500 g organic coconut milk
1 tsp agar-agar powder
1 tsp vanilla sugar
1 pinch salt
For the topping:
300 g strawberries
1 tbsp sugar
1 tbsp lemon juice
a few mint leaves
PREPERATION

For the panna cotta, bring the coconut milk , sugar, vanilla and salt to the boil in a saucepan. Mix the
agar agar powder with a little cold water and stir into the coconut milk. Let the
coconut milk boil for at least 2 minutes so that the agar agar gels when it cools. Stir
occasionally. Pour the coconut milk into small glasses and keep cool.
Wash the strawberries and remove the stems.

If desired, puree half of the fruit with the sugar and lemon juice and cut the remaining strawberries
into pieces. Cut the mint leaves into fine strips and fold them into the strawberries. Divide the
strawberry puree between the glasses and top with the strawberry pieces. Or cut all the strawberries
into pieces, mix them with sugar and lemon juice and distribute them among the glasses.

INGREDIENTS
125g soft butter
150g sugar
1 pack vanilla sugar
2 eggs
180g flour
1 pinch salt
2 tbsp cocoa powder
2 tsp baking powder
75 ml milk
12 oreo cookies
For the frosting:
200 g cream
100 g soft butter

150 g powdered sugar
18 oreo cookies
PREPERATION

For the dough, mix butter with both types of sugar. Stir in eggs. Preheat the oven to 180 degrees
(fan oven: 160 degrees) . Mix the flour with salt, cocoa powder and baking powder and stir into the dough alternately
with the milk. Roughly chop the Oreo cookies and fold them into the dough. Line the wells
of a muffin tin with muffin cups. Use an ice cream scoop to distribute the dough among the molds.
Bake in the preheated oven for approx. 25 minutes. Allow to cool completely.

For the frosting, whip the cream until stiff and set aside. Mix butter with cream cheese and powdered

cream. Place the cream in a piping bag fitted with a star nozzle. Tighten the top of the piping bag
tightly. Then pipe a cream topping onto each cupcake.

For decoration, prepare the remaining 12 Oreo cookies and place an Oreo cookie on each cupcake.
Refrigerate until serving.

INGREDIENTS
226 g unsalted butter
250 g granulated sugar
100 g brown sugar
4 eggs
1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
354 g flour
1 tbsp baking powder
1 tsp salt
240 ml buttermilk
2 tbsp lemonn zest
120 ml lemon juice
210 g blueberries
1
For the frosting:
226 g cream cheese
113 g unsalted butter
420 g confectioners’ sugar
15-30 ml heavy cream
1 tbsp pure vanilla extract
pinch salt
PREPERATION

Make the cake:Using a handheld or stand mixer with paddle attachment, beat the butter on high until.
Add granulated and brown sugars and beat on medium speed until creamed,about 2 minutes.Add
eggs and vanilla. Beat on medium speed until everything is completely combined, about 2 minutes.
Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt. Slowly add the dry ingredients
to the wet ingredients. Beat on low speed for 5 seconds, then beat in the milk, lemon zest, and
lemon juice *just* until combined. Toss the blueberries with 1 Tablespoon of flour and gently
fold into the batter. Batter is extremely thick. Do not over-mix. Over-mixing will lead to a
tough, dense textured crumb.

Spoon batter evenly into prepared cake pans. Bake for about 21-25 minutes or until a toothpick
inserted in the center comes out clean. (8 inch cakes take closer to 25 minutes.) Remove from
the oven and allow to cool completely in the pan before assembling and frosting.

Make the frosting: Using a handheld or stand mixer with a paddle attachment, beat cream cheese
and butter together on medium speed until no lumps remain, about 3 full minutes. Add
confectioners’ sugar, 1 Tablespoon cream, vanilla extract, and salt with the mixer running on
low. Turn mixer to high speed and beat for 3 minutes. Add 1 more Tablespoon of cream to thin
out, if desired.

Assemble and frost: First, using a large serrated knife, trim the tops off the cake layers to
create a flat surface. Place 1 layer on your cake stand. Evenly cover the top with cream cheese
frosting. Top with 2nd layer, more frosting, then the third layer. Top with frosting and spread
around the sides. The recipe doesn’t make a ton of frosting, just enough for a light frost.
Top with blueberries or lemon garnish if desired. Refrigerate for at least 45 minutes before
cutting or else the cake may fall apart as you cut.

INGREDIENTS

for 12 cupcakes

100 g dark chocolate
300 g butter
4 tsp instant coffee
90 g flour
1 tbsp cocoa powder
80 g sugar
1 bag vanilla sugar
1 tsp baking powder
salt
3 eggs
150 g cream cheese
80 g powdered sugar
16-24 Chocolate mocha beans
PREPERATION

Crush the chocolate and melt it in a saucepan with 180 g butter and 2 teaspoons of instant coffee over low
heat while stirring. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.Preheat the oven to 180°. Mix flour, cocoa, sugar,
vanilla sugar, baking powder and a pinch of salt. First stir the eggs and then the flour mixture into the
chocolate mixture.

Line a muffin tray with 12 paper cases, spread the dough on them and bake in a hot oven(centre,fan oven 160°)
for 20-25 minutes.For the mocha cream, mix the remaining instant coffee with 1 tablespoon of hot water and
allow to cool. Beat the remaining butter with the powdered sugar until fluffy. Gradually stir in the cream
cheese and finally the coffee. Refrigerate the finished cream until ready to use.

Take the cupcakes out of the oven, let them cool slightly, remove them from the tin and let them cool
completely. Fill the mocha cream into a piping bag with a star nozzle and pipe it onto the cakes.

INGREDIENTS
80 g butter
90 g powdered sugar
175 g peanut butter with pieces
8 biscuits
250 g couverture, whole milk
1 tbsp vegetable oil, neutral
paper cases
PREPERATION

Melt butter, powdered sugar and peanut butter in a saucepan over low heat until thick. Remove from heat.  
Crush the cookies (e.g. put them in a freezer bag and roll them into small pieces with a rolling pin),
add them to the peanut mixture and stir.

Pour the still warm and liquid mixture into the paper molds about 1 cm high (two paper molds, one
inside the other, ensure greater stability) and let the “blanks” solidify in the freezer for about
half an hour. For the coating, melt the couverture in a water bath according to the package
instructions and finally add 1 tablespoon of neutral vegetable oil.

Remove the cooled peanut mixture from the paper molds and pour approx. 1 - 1.5 teaspoons of liquid
couverture into the same paper molds and, by turning the molds slightly at an angle, let the
couverture flow up the edge (as high as the peanut filling). Place the peanut blank on the
still liquid chocolate and cover the top with chocolate.

for 12 muffins

PREPERATION

Mix the flour with Backin in a mixing bowl. Add the remaining ingredients, except the berries,

INGREDIENTS
oil, for greasing
6 eggs
100 g yoghurt
50 ml milk
450 g butter
450 g sugar
450g flour
zest of lemon,plus juice
3 tbsp elderflower cordial
For the icing:
250g butter
300g cream cheese
zest of 1 lemon
flowers to decorate
PREPERATION

In a jug, whisk eggs, yogurt and milk.Beat the butter and sugar together in a large bowl, using an
electric hand whisk.When you have a light and fluffy mixture, add the flour, liquid in the jug and
the lemon zest. Mix again until smooth.Divide the cake mixture between the tins, level the surfaces
and bake for 40 mins.

Mix the lemon juice and elderflower cordial. When the cakes are cooked, poke all over the surface
with a cocktail stick then spoon the lemon and elderflower syrup over the cakes. Leave to cool in
the tins. Once cool, you can wrap in cling film and keep for 3 days before icing.

To make the icing, beat the butter until smooth with an electric hand whisk. Add half the icing
sugar, use a spatula to mash the mixture together(this will help to prevent an icing sugar cloud)
then whisk again. Add the remaining icing sugar, cream cheese and lemon zest, mash again, then
whisk again until smooth.

Stack the cakes on a cake stand with plenty of icing between each layer. Pile most of the
remaining icing on top, then use a palette knife to spread it across the top and down the
sides, covering the cake in swirls (don’t worry about it looking too perfect). Add the final
bit of icing to the top and use it to cover any patches where the cake is poking through.
Decorate with fresh flowers.

INGREDIENTS
1 Sheet Puff Pastry
For the pastry cream:
¾ cup of whole milk
2 large egg yolks
½ vanilla bean
2 tbsp corn starch
1 tbsp unsalted butter
⅓ cup heavy whipping cream
½ cup granulated sugar
Roasted Strawberry filling:
1 cup fresh strawberries
2 tbsp granulated sugar
PREPERATION

Place strawberries on baking sheet & sprinkle sugar,toss to combine.Roast strawberries
for 15 minutes or until softened and syrupy. Cool completely.Thaw your puff pastry on
the counter for about 30 minutes, meanwhile, prepare the pastry cream.

In a medium-sized bowl, whisk sugar & cornstarch until well-combined.Add eggyolks
and mix well. Set aside. In a medium saucepan, heat milk and vanilla until simmering.
Slowly add the heated milk mixture to the egg yolks, whisking constantly, and ensure
the egg yolks do not curdle. Return the mixture to the saucepan. On medium heat,
whisking constantly, for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until mixture is thick and
bubbly. Remove from heat and whisk in butter. Strain the mixture into a shallow dish
and cover directly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate until fully chilled.

Place the puff pastry onto a lightly floured surface. Roll out to about 12”x12” and
use a pizza cutter to cut 2x4 inch rectangles.Line a baking sheet with parchment
paper then place the rectangles about 2 inches apart. Place another sheet of
parchment on top then cover with another baking sheet.Bake for about 15 minutes

With a hand mixer, whip the heavy whipping cream. Once the pastry cream is fully
cooled, fold the whipped cream into the pastry cream gently to combine. Transfer
the mixture to a pastry bag and snip a ¼-inch opening from the end.Place one
layer of puff pastry on a platter. Pipe dots of filling all over the pastry,
then top with a tablespoon of strawberry filling. Then top with another layer
of the rectangle. Repeat the process one more time. Dust the latest sheet with
powdered sugar.

INGREDIENTS
For the dough:
250 g margarine
200 g sugar
1 pack vanilla sugar
5 eggs
250 g wheat flour
2 teaspoons baking soda
2 teaspoons baking cocoa
75 ml milk
100 g dark chocolate
For the filling:
100 g cream
400g cream cheese
125g soft butter
100 g powdered sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
For the topping:
100 g cream
60g dark chocolate
PREPERATION

For the dough, mix margarine with sugar and vanilla sugar. Gradually stir in eggs. Mix flour with baking
powder and cocoa powder. Add to the margarine-sugar mixture alternately with the milk and stir in.
Finely chop the dark chocolate and fold it into the dough.

Preheat the oven to 180 degrees top/bottom heat (fan oven: 160 degrees). Grease a springform pan
(Ø 20 cm), then fill it with the dough and smooth it out. Bake in the preheated oven for approx.
60 minutes. Allow to cool completely, then remove from the mold.

For the filling, whip the cream until stiff and refrigerate until ready to use. Beat cream cheese
with butter, powdered sugar and vanilla extract for several minutes until creamy. It is important
that the butter and cream cheese are at room temperature so that the ingredients combine well and
a smooth cream is created. Finally, fold in the cooled, whipped cream.

Cut the dough into three equal-sized layers. Place the first layer on the cake plate and add 2-3
tablespoons of cream on top. Smooth it out and lay down the next floor. Continue like this until
all the floors are on top of each other. Now spread the entire cake with the remaining cream. If
possible, the floors should no longer be visible, especially at the edge. Refrigerate the cake
for at least 30 minutes.

In the meantime, put the cream in a saucepan for the topping. Roughly chop the dark chocolate,
bring the cream to the boil briefly, then add the chocolate to the cream. Remove pan from heat
and stir frequently until chocolate has melted. Now let the chocolate sauce cool down until it
is only lukewarm but still liquid (approx. 20-30 minutes).
When the sauce has cooled, take the cake out of the fridge. Use a teaspoon to drizzle some of
the chocolate sauce down the edge of the cake. Continue until chocolate drops run down the
entire edge of the cake. Now cover the top of the cake with chocolate.

INGREDIENTS

for 12 tartlets

For the dough:  
195 g flour
2 ½ tbsp sugar
113 g unsalted butter
1 egg yolk
1 – 2 tbsp iced water
For the lemon filling:
100 g white granulated sugar
25 g cornflour
pinch of salt
125 ml lemon juice
160 ml water
28 g unsalted butter
grated zest of lemon
3 egg yolks
PREPERATION

Place the flour, icing sugar and chilled butter into the bowl of a food processor.Blitz to
crumb-like texture.Add the egg yolk and blitz again until well combined. With the processor
running, slowly drizzle in the water, just enough until it starts clumping together.Pull the
pastry dough into a smooth ball (try not to knead too much). Roll out between two sheets of
baking paper to 3mm thick, then place on a tray or flat surface in the fridge to chill
for ½ an hour.

Preheat oven to 180C / 350F / 160C fan forced.Prick the bases of the tartlet shells a few
times with a fork. Place a cupcake case or piece of baking paper into each, pressing it to
the pastry at the bottom, and fill with rice (or pie weights).Transfer to the preheated oven
and bake for 15 minutes then remove the cupcake cases and bake a further 5 minutes.
Set aside

FOR THE LEMON CURD

Combine sugar, cornflour and salt in a saucepan, then pour in the lemon juice and water.
Whisk well to combine. Cook, whisking regularly so you don’t get lumps, until you have
a thick gel with big bubbles popping all over the surface.Take it off the heat and
add butter and zest, mix until melted and fully combined.
While whisking vigorously, tip in the yolks and whisk well until fully combined

Place the saucepan back over the heat and cook for a further minute whisking
frequently.Divide the curd evenly among the cases – each should be full pretty much to
the top. Bake a further 5-8 minutes until the curd is level and just starting to bubble.
Let them cool 15 minutes in the muffin tin, then gently give each one a twist and lift
it out. Finish cooling on a wire rack.

INGREDIENTS
2 large eggs
1/4 cup (55 g) plain 2% Greek yogurt
2 medium-size, bananas, mashed
6 Tbsp (75 g) coconut palm sugar
1 tsp vanilla extract
1 tsp baking soda
1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
1/4 salt
1 1/2 cup (180 g) whole wheat pastry flour
1 cup (140 g) blueberries, fresh or frozen
PREPERATION

Preheat your oven to 350ºF (176ºC), and lightly grease a 9 x 5 (23cm x 13cm) bread pan.

In a large mixing bowl, lightly beat the eggs until the yolks break apart. Whisk in the
yogurt, bananas, sugar, vanilla, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt, mixing until smooth.
Spoon in the flour and gently fold it in until just combined. Toss the blueberries in 1
Tbsp (7 g) of flour to prevent them from bleeding or sinking to the bottom of the
muffins, and gently fold them into the batter.

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan, spreading it out evenly. Bake for
45 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

Remove the bread from the oven and let it cool in the pan for about 10 minutes
before transferring it to a cooling rack to cool completely. Store in an airtight
container at room temperature for up to 5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.

For the cake:
1 3/4 c. granulated sugar
1 3/4 c. all-purpose flour
3/4 c. cocoa powder
1/2 tsp. kosher salt
1 1/2 tsp. baking soda
1 1/2 tsp. baking powder
2 large eggs, lightly beaten
1 tsp. vanilla extract
1/2 c. vegetable oil
3/4 c. milk
3/4 c. hot (not boiling) water
1/4 c. raspberry preserves
2 6-oz. fresh raspberries, divided
2 tbsp. chopped roasted pistachios
Shaved chocolate curls, opt
For the buttercream:
1 1/2 c. unsalted butter
4 1/2 c. confectioners' sugar
2/3 c. cocoa powder
1/3 c. heavy whipping cream
1 1/2 tsp. vanilla extract
1/4 tsp kosher salt
PREPERATION

For the cake: Preheat the oven to 350°. Line 8-inch round baking pans with parchment
paper, then spray with nonstick baking spray with flour.
(Alternatively, butter and flour the cake pans.)

Whisk together the sugar, flour, cocoa powder, salt, baking soda, and baking powder in
a large bowl.

Whisk together the eggs, vanilla, oil, and milk in a medium bowl. Add the egg mixture
to the flour mixture and whisk well to combine. Scrape down the sides and bottom of
the bowl to be sure all the ingredients get mixed together and there are no clumps of
batter. Add the hot water and whisk well to combine (the batter will be thin and soupy).

Pour the batter into the prepared pans and bake for 25 to 30 minutes, or until a
wooden pick inserted near the center comes out clean. Cool in the pans for 10
minutes, then remove and cool completely on a wire rack.

For the buttercream: Beat the butter on medium speed in the mixing bowl of a
stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment until creamy, about 2 minutes.
Add the confectioners' sugar and beat until smooth. Sift the cocoa powder
over the buttercream then beat on low speed until all the cocoa is absorbed.
Add the heavy cream, vanilla extract and salt and beat on medium until
mixture is lightened and creamy, about 2 more minutes.

To assemble the cake, spread the raspberry preserves over 1 cake layer.
Spread about 1 cup of buttercream over the preserves. Scatter half of
the raspberries onto the buttercream and press them gently into the
cream. Place the other layer upside down on top of the buttercream
and raspberries. Spread the remaining buttercream onto the sides
and top of the cake

Decorate the top of the cake with the remaining raspberries.Sprinkle with
pistachios and shave chocolate over top, if you like. Refrigerate
or store in a cool place until serving.

INGREDIENTS
For the puree:
2 c. blueberries
2 tbsp. granulated sugar
2 tsp. lemon juice
For the crust:
9 graham crackers, finely crushed
5 tbsp. butter, melted
1/4 c. granulated sugar
For the cake:
4 blocks cream cheese, softened
1 c. granulated sugar
2 large eggs
1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
1/4 c. sour cream
2 tbsp. all-purpose flour
1/4 tsp. kosher salt
1 c. blueberry puree
For the topping:
- Whipped cream
- Blueberries
PREPERATION

Preheat oven to 325°.In a small food processor,blend blueberries until no large chunks
remain.

In a small saucepan over medium heat, add blueberry puree, sugar, and
lemon juice. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer until slightly reduced,
stirring occasionally, 10 minutes. Let cool to room temperature.

Make crust: In a large bowl, mix together graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and
sugar, until mixture resembles wet sand. Press into bottom and up sides of an 8" or
9" springform pan.

Make cheesecake: In a large bowl using a hand mixer (or the bowl of a stand mixer),
beat cream cheese and sugar until no lumps remain. Add eggs, one at a time, then
stir in vanilla and sour cream. Add flour and salt and beat until just combined.
Fold in blueberry puree.

Pour mixture over crust. Wrap bottom of pan in aluminum foil and place in a large
roasting pan. Pour in enough boiling water to come halfway up the baking pan.

Bake until center of cheesecake only slightly jiggles, about 1 hour 30 minutes.
Turn off heat, prop open oven door, and let cheesecake cool in oven, 1 hour.

Remove foil and refrigerate cheesecake until completely chilled, at least 5 hours
and up to overnight.

When ready to serve, top cheesecake with whipped cream and blueberries.

spread the icing on the cake.
